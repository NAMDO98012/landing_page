Pokemon Games

Pokemon Main Series Game contain

Game Boy(Generation I): Pokémon Red and Blue: Introduced the original 151 Pokémon in the Kanto region. Pokémon Yellow: Special Pikachu Edition, based on the animated series.

Game Boy Color (Generation II): Pokémon Gold and Silver: Added 100 new Pokémon and introduced breeding and day-night cycles. Pokémon Crystal: Enhanced version with animated sprites and additional story elements.

Game Boy Advance (Generation III): Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire: Set in the Hoenn region with new mechanics like Abilities and Double Battles. Pokémon Emerald: Enhanced version with additional storyline involving Team Aqua and Team Magma.

Pokémon Diamond and Pearl: Introduced the Sinnoh region and the concept of the Distortion World. Featured the legendary Pokémon Dialga and Palkia. Pokémon Platinum: Enhanced version with the Battle Frontier, expanded storyline involving Giratina and the Distortion World, and improvements to gameplay and graphics. Pokémon HeartGold and SoulSilver: Remakes of Pokémon Gold and Silver, featuring the Johto and Kanto regions, following the player's journey to become the Pokémon Champion

Pokémon Black and White: Introduced the Unova region, 156 new Pokémon, and the storyline focused on the conflict between Team Plasma and ideals of Pokémon liberation. Pokémon Black 2 and White 2: Sequels set two years after Black and White, featuring new locations and an expanded Unova Pokédex. Pokémon X and Y: Set in the Kalos region, introduced 3D graphics, Mega Evolution, and the storyline involving Team Flare and the legendary Pokémon Xerneas and Yveltal. Pokémon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire: Remakes of Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire, introducing Mega Evolution and featuring the Hoenn region with updated graphics and gameplay mechanics.

Nintendo Switch (Generation VII and VIII): Pokémon Sun and Moon: Set in the Alola region, introduced Alolan forms, Z-Moves, and the Aether Foundation's storyline. Pokémon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon: Alternate storyline set in Alola, featuring new Ultra Beasts and enhancements to gameplay mechanics. Pokémon Sword and Shield: Set in the Galar region, introduced Dynamax and Gigantamax forms, Wild Area exploration, and the Isle of Armor and Crown Tundra expansions.

Review and Ranking worst to best games of the main series

  1. Pokémon Red and Blue (Generation I): These games introduced Pokémon to the world and set the foundation for the series. They hold significant nostalgic value and are often remembered fondly by long-time fans.
  2. Pokémon Gold and Silver (Generation II): Known for expanding upon the original games with new features like breeding and day-night cycles. They are highly regarded for their gameplay improvements and nostalgic value.
  3. Pokémon Emerald (Generation III): Considered one of the best versions of the Hoenn region games, with an expanded storyline and the introduction of the Battle Frontier, which added depth to post-game content.
  4. Pokémon HeartGold and SoulSilver (Generation IV): Remakes of the classic Gold and Silver games, praised for their faithful recreation of Johto and Kanto, as well as the Pokéwalker.
  5. Pokémon FireRed and LeafGreen (Generation III): Enhanced remakes of the original Red and Blue games, introducing modernized graphics and gameplay improvements while maintaining the nostalgic charm.
  6. Pokémon Black and White (Generation V): Noted for their story-driven approach, new Pokémon designs, and innovations like the seasons mechanic. They received praise for their narrative depth and engaging gameplay.
  7. Pokémon Platinum (Generation IV): Enhanced version of Diamond and Pearl, known for its expanded storyline, improved gameplay mechanics, and introduction of the Distortion World.
  8. Pokémon X and Y (Generation VI): These games marked the transition to 3D graphics and introduced Mega Evolution, which added a new layer of strategy to battles. They were praised for their visual style and gameplay innovations.
  9. Pokémon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire (Generation VI): Remakes of Ruby and Sapphire with updated graphics and gameplay mechanics like the DexNav and the Delta Episode, which expanded the storyline.
  10. Pokémon Sun and Moon (Generation VII): Introducing the Alola region, Alolan forms, Z-Moves, and a departure from the traditional Gym system in favor of Trials. They were praised for their fresh take on the series.
  11. Pokémon Sword and Shield (Generation VIII): Set in the Galar region and introducing Dynamax and Gigantamax forms. These games generated mixed reactions due to controversies surrounding Pokémon cuts and graphical fidelity.
  12. Pokémon Yellow: Special Pikachu Edition (Generation I): Based on the animated series, it added Pikachu as a starter Pokémon and included elements inspired by the TV show.
  13. Pokémon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon (Generation VII): Alternate storyline versions of Sun and Moon with additional features and Pokémon. They received mixed reception for being iterative rather than innovative.

This ranking is based on people preference and opinion

Game Information

The Pokémon main game series, developed by Game Freak and published by Nintendo, has been a cornerstone of Nintendo's handheld and console gaming platforms since its debut in 1996. Pokemon main series game is about role playing as a pokemon trainer. The main goal of the game is to catch pokemon and battle againist other trainers

core game play mechanics

Battle System: Pokémon battles are turn-based and typically involve strategies based on type matchups and move effectiveness. Exploration: Players navigate through various towns, cities, and routes, encountering Pokémon in the wild and other Trainers to battle. Pokédex: A digital encyclopedia that records information about Pokémon species encountered and caught.
